GENERAL INFORMATION

It is an important vegetable crop of India. These are mother fruits of several better-known cultivars. Muskmelon is native to Iran, Anatolia and Armenia. Musk melon is a rich source of Vitamin A and Vitamin C. It contains about 90% of water and 9% Carbohydrates. In India muskmelon growing states include Punjab, Tamil Nadu, Uttar Pradesh, Maharashtra, and Andhra Pradesh.

SOIL

It grows well in deep fertile and well-drained soil. It gives best results when grown on well drained loam soil. Soil having poor drainage capacity is not suited for Muskmelon cultivation. Follow crop rotation as continuous growing of the same crop on the same field leads to loss of nutrients, poor yield and more disease attacks. pH of soil should be in between 6-7. Alkaline soil with high salt concentration is not suitable for cultivation.

POPULAR VARIETIES WITH THEIR YIELD

Hara Madhu: Late maturing variety. Fruits are of large size, round shape having average weight of about one kg. Skin is of light yellow color. TSS contains about 13% and is very sweet in tests. Flesh is green color, thick and juicy. Seeds are of small size. It is resistant to powdery mildew. It gives an average yield of 50 qtl/acre.

Punjab Sunehri: Mature 12 days before Hara Madhu. Fruits are of round shape with netted rind and of light brown color. Its average weight is about 700-800gm, TSS is about 11%. Flesh is thick, orange. It has good keeping quality. It is resistant to fruit attacks. Its average yield is about 65qtl/acre.

MH-51: Released in 2017. It gives an average yield of 89qtl/acre. It has round fruits, has stripes and is gauzed. It contains 12% sucrose content.

MH-27: Released in 2015. It gives an average yield of 88 qtl/acre. It contains 12.5% sucrose content.

LAND PREPARATION

Plough land and bring to fine tilth. In North India, sowing is done in the middle of February month. In North east and west India sowing is done during November to January

SOWING

Time of sowing Middle of February is the optimum time for muskmelon cultivation.

Spacing Prepare 3-4m wide beds depending upon variety use. Sow two seeds per hill on the bed and keep a distance of 60cm between the hill.

Method of sowing For Sowing, dibbling methods and transplanting methods can be used.

Sow seeds in a polythene bag of 15cm x 12cm size with thickness of 100 gauge in the last week of January or first week of February. Fill a polythene bag with equal proportion of well rotted cow dung and soil. Seedlings are ready for transplantation by the end of February or first week of March. Transplantation is done for 25-30 days old seedlings. Apply irrigation immediately after transplantation.

SEED

Seed Rate For sowing in one acre land, a seed rate of 400 gm is required.

Seed Treatment Before sowing, treat the seed with Carbendazim@2gm/kg of seeds. After chemical treatment, treat the seeds with Trichoderma viride@4gm per kg of seeds. Dry seeds in shade and then do sowing immediately.

WEED CONTROL

Keep bed weed free during the early stage of growth. In absence of proper control measures, weed can cause yield loss of 30%. 15-20 days after sowing carry out intercultural operations. Depending upon severity and intensity of weeds, two to three weeding are required.

IRRIGATION

Apply irrigation, every week in the summer season. At time of maturity give irrigation only when needed. Avoid over flooding in the muskmelon field. During application of irrigation, do not wet the vines or vegetative parts, especially during flowering and fruit-set. Avoid frequent irrigation in heavy soil as it will promote excessive vegetative growth. For better sweetness and flavor, stop irrigation or reduce watering 3-6 days before harvesting.
